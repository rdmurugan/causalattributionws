Why Causal Attribution?

Traditional attribution methods rely on correlation, which often leads to misattribution and wasted marketing spend. Our causal inference approach reveals true cause-and-effect relationships in your customer journeys.

Causal Inference vs. Correlation

The Problem with Correlation-Based Attribution

Most attribution platforms use correlation-based models (like last-click, first-click, or simple multi-touch attribution). These models can't distinguish between:

  • Causation: Channel A truly drove the conversion
  • Correlation: Channel A just happened to occur before conversion
  • Confounding: Both Channel A and conversion were caused by a third factor

The Power of Causal Inference

Our API uses advanced causal inference methods to discover true cause-and-effect relationships:

  • PC Algorithm: Discovers causal structure from observational data
  • Propensity Score Matching: Accounts for selection bias in channel exposure
  • Doubly Robust Estimation: Combines multiple estimation strategies for robustness
  • Instrumental Variables: Identifies causal effects even with hidden confounders
